The Shocking Truths our Survey Reveals About Affluent Indian Households!

Survey Context and Motivation

• Alarming RBI data: Net household financial savings at 50-year low (barely 5%), while household debt excluding home loans at 33-34% of income
• Stock market paradox: DMAT accounts grew 6X from 30M to 180M in 5 years, but income taxpayers remained at 30M
• AI disruption concerns: Growing worry about AI's impact on middle-class jobs with repetitive tasks
• Survey scope: 456 affluent Indians (post-tax income ≥20 lakhs) aged 30+ in metros and tier 1/2 cities

Critical Savings Crisis Among Affluent Indians

• Insufficient savings: 43% of affluent Indians save less than 20% of their income
• Worst performing age group: 50% of Indians aged 30-45 (prime earning years) don't save even 20%
• Zero emergency funds: 14% of affluent respondents have no emergency savings whatsoever
• Misaligned expectations: Despite low savings, Indians maintain oversized dreams and financial goals

Unrealistic Financial Aspirations

• Multiple big goals: 40% want houses, 40% want to start businesses, 40% want early retirement
• Family obligations: 75% saving for children's education and marriage expenses
• Hedonic treadmill effect: As income rises, aspirations scale up disproportionately without proper goal classification
• Essential vs luxury confusion: Many treat luxury goals (holiday homes) as essential as retirement planning

Poor Asset Allocation Strategies

• Real estate obsession: Despite only 4-5% returns over 10 years, real estate remains the largest allocation beyond primary residence
• Equity underallocation: Only 20% of affluent Indian wealth invested in equities despite 5-year bull market
• Tax-inefficient choices: Heavy allocation to illiquid, low-return, tax-unfriendly real estate over inflation-beating assets
• Long-term underperformance: Real estate hasn't beaten inflation over 10-40 year cycles

Financial Advisory System Failures

• Widespread dissatisfaction: Two-thirds of respondents unhappy with financial advice received
• Generic advice problem: One-third complain advice isn't personalized to their unique situations and needs
• Limited advisor knowledge: Only 13% comfortable with their savings and investment knowledge
• Fragmented advice sources: 32% rely on CAs/wealth managers, 25% on friends/family, 20% on bank managers

Retirement Planning Reality Check

• Massive funding gap: A 40-year-old earning 30 lakhs needs 5 crores in today's money for same lifestyle in retirement
• Mathematical impossibility: Current savings rates insufficient to meet stated retirement and lifestyle goals
• Urgent intervention needed: Gap between aspirations and savings capacity requires immediate financial planning
