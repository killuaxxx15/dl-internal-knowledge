Shopify President on Ambition Gone Too Far: "How I Burned Out at the Top"

Early Family Influence and Immigrant Roots

• His family background was shaped by his grandparents’ survival of the Holocaust and immigration to Canada during the Hungarian Revolution, arriving with no money or education.
• His father embodied "immigrant ambition," wanting his children to have a better life despite financial struggles masked by a sense of abundance at home.
• At 17, he faced a formative trauma when his father was arrested for white-collar crimes, shattering his sense of security and forcing him to grow up quickly.
• The experience left lasting emotional impact, driving feelings of scarcity, ambition, competitiveness, and anxiety about never being enough.
• Despite early anger and disappointment, he later rebuilt a loving relationship with his father, recognizing the sacrifices made for the family.

The Formation of High Standards and Ambition

• Moving to South Florida as a teenager exposed him to stark wealth disparities, igniting a “chip on his shoulder” and feelings of being below average.
• Early ambition manifested in high school achievements such as becoming student council president and a varsity wrestler despite being a scared, out-of-place newcomer.
• His father encouraged entrepreneurial instincts by supporting his DJ business with personalized business cards, empowering his confidence despite financial limitations.
• He credited his father’s unwavering support—even through personal failures like prison—with instilling both deep ambition and a drive to achieve.
• Recognizes both positive and negative externalities from this upbringing: constant anxiety and drive to do more, alongside high energy and excitement for life.

Relationship with Ambition and Personal Standards

• Admitted to having unreasonably high standards for himself professionally, often struggling with a scarcity mindset despite success.
• Holds equally high standards in relationships, valuing quality and depth in friendships but acknowledging that such standards can lead to disappointment.
• Discusses the burden of multi-generational trauma, believing it shaped his mindset around achievement, self-worth, and constant striving.
• Sees entrepreneurship as the great equalizer, aligning his personal mission with Shopify’s goal of making entrepreneurship accessible.
• Describes ambition as both a motivator and a source of stress, recognizing the duality in its impact on his life.

Philosophy on Prioritization and Intentional Living

• Emphasizes intentionality in all aspects of life, encapsulated by his family motto: “How you do anything is how you do everything.”
• Recognizes the impossibility of being world-class at everything, drawing an analogy to character selection in Street Fighter where one must choose strengths and weaknesses.
• Made peace with prioritization, understanding that saying no to some things is essential to avoid mediocrity.
• Advocates for being deliberate about how, where, and with whom one spends time, believing people and places can be either energy catalysts or energy vampires.
• Highlights Shopify’s culture of “spiky objects,” encouraging team members to be the very best in their specific domain rather than well-rounded generalists.

The Concept of Requalifying for Your Role

• Describes Shopify’s expectation for leaders to “requalify” for their roles annually, rejecting stasis in favor of continuous improvement.
• Explains the “trust battery” metaphor, where autonomy depends on maintaining trust through ongoing performance.
• Credits CEO Toby Lütke with setting relentless standards for improvement and seeing a better version of him than he did himself.
• Details how praise at Shopify is reserved for exceeding expectations and pushing the boundaries of industry norms.
• Emphasizes the idea of moving from “local maxima” (being better than peers) to “global maxima” (completely redefining what great looks like).

Transition from COO to Chief Storyteller

• Acknowledges his time as COO felt unnatural despite operational success, draining his energy and excitement.
• Initially resisted admitting storytelling was his true strength, fearing it wasn’t “enough” and came with less perceived importance.
• Credits Toby for giving him permission to embrace the role of storyteller, recognizing its value to Shopify’s mission.
• Describes the new role as a perfect fit for his energy, passion, and unique value-add, revitalizing his commitment to Shopify.
• Frames the transition as aligning with the concept of “Ikigai,” finding work that he loves, is valued, and is uniquely skilled at.

The Craft and Practice of Storytelling

• Defines storytelling as the area where he wants to be truly world-class, believing it’s his greatest value to Shopify.
• Describes developing the skill through extensive practice, including watching and critiquing his early podcast performances to eliminate filler words.
• Warns against copying others’ styles, instead advocating for authentic storytelling rooted in personal experience and conviction.
• Credits mentorship from renowned storytellers like Seth Godin for refining his approach.
• Highlights the importance of personal stories in creating compelling narratives, aligning his life story with Shopify’s entrepreneurial mission.

Building a Personal Board of Mentors

• Advocates for having mentors tailored to specific life areas rather than adopting all traits from one person.
• Describes mentors as “spiky objects,” exceptional in some areas but often flawed in others, requiring selective learning.
• Emphasizes the importance of evolving mentorship relationships over time, recognizing when a mentor’s value has plateaued.
• Shares how he and his wife identified and spent time with couples they admired to develop relationship mentorship.
• Suggests adopting a “zero cringe” approach to reaching out for mentorship, overcoming fear of rejection to build meaningful connections.

Teaching His Children Through Example

• Reflects on the challenge of raising children without replicating the hardship that shaped his own drive.
• Emphasizes modeling gratitude, respect, and hard work through everyday behaviors rather than manufactured scarcity.
• Encourages his daughters’ involvement in entrepreneurial settings like Shopify’s Builder Sundays, hoping to instill curiosity and problem-solving.
• Aims to show them that success requires intentionality, perseverance, and learning from failures rather than entitlement.
• Rejects passive acceptance encapsulated in the phrase “it is what it is,” advocating instead for changing one’s circumstances with deliberate action.

The Role of Ambition, Care, and High Agency

• Equates caring deeply with ambition, valuing those who out-care others as having a superpower in business and life.
• Believes ambition can be developed but views innate, deep-rooted desire to succeed as crucial.
• Advises others to align work with what they truly care about, arguing that passion fuels skill development and resilience.
• Sees failure tolerance and willingness to look foolish temporarily as critical to long-term mastery.
• Encourages his children to observe and emulate high-agency behaviors, emphasizing responsibility for outcomes and rejecting fatalism.
