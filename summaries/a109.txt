Early Life and Singapore's Economic Model

• Grew up with nomadic existence due to father's Navy postings, including critical years in Singapore under Lee Kuan Yew
• Singapore provided stark contrast to India's socialism - gave people maximum economic freedom while maintaining political control
• Singapore's success came from no welfare state, massive infrastructure investment, and ruthless competition
• Found passion for economics early, started writing financial columns at age 18 while hiding that he was still an undergraduate

East Asian Growth Model vs India's Constraints

• East Asian countries (China, Singapore, Korea) achieved 8-9% growth by firing millions from state enterprises and eliminating welfare states
• China fired 90 million people from state-owned enterprises in the 1990s with no safety net, forcing migration to productive coastal cities
• India unlikely to ever achieve sustained 9-10% growth due to democratic constraints and inability to implement such "ruthless" capitalism
• India reconciled to 6% growth rate due to political and social limitations around welfare and labor mobility

Social Mobility and Economic Policy Recommendations

• Key to social mobility: equal opportunity (not outcomes), no government bailouts, democratized education, no premature welfare state
• Deregulation critical - every new regulation typically favors incumbents and hurts new entrants
• Home affordability crisis in West caused by supply restrictions from regulation, not demand issues
• India needs "DOGE" (Department of Government Efficiency) for deregulation, potentially led by people like Nandan Nilekani or business leaders

Anti-Incumbency and Political Trends

• Western democracies now show strong anti-incumbency (most governments lose elections), while emerging markets show opposite trend
• This reflects declining social mobility and economic satisfaction in developed countries vs improving conditions in emerging markets
• Americans' belief in having better life than parents dropped from 70-80% in 1950s-60s to 30-40% today
• Political anger in West driven by reduced economic and social mobility over past 30-40 years

Corruption and Regulation

• Corruption levels generally correlate with per capita income - India not unusually corrupt for its income level
• Distinction between "efficient" vs "inefficient" corruption - China gets things done after payment, India often doesn't
• Excessive regulation forces businesses to violate laws to operate, creating artificial corruption
• India needs amnesty approach like China in 1990s - charge penalty but allow people to move forward

Foreign Investment and Capital Controls

• India's FDI consistently below 1% of GDP vs 3-4% in successful East Asian countries
• Main barriers: difficult regulatory environment, fear of investigative agencies, complex business setup process
• Capital account convertibility would likely bring net inflows, not outflows, but policymaker fear prevents implementation
• Need separate body for FDI attraction and seamless regulator interactions

US Economic Outlook and AI Bubble

• US outperformance of last 15 years likely ending - rest of world should outperform next 5-10 years
• Only reason to invest in US today is AI; everything else overvalued or fundamentally flawed
• AI bubble has echoes of 1999-2000 - good idea gone too far with unsustainable valuations
• Dollar weakness expected as reserve currency status gradually erodes, accelerated by Russia sanctions precedent

China's Current State

• Two major negatives: excessive debt (now at US levels despite lower income) and shrinking population
• Major positive: technological prowess remains impressive, only country that can match US in AI development
• Most competitive market in the world due to overcapacity - harder for investors to make money than in US
• Success came from government getting out of the way for 30 years, opposite of common perception

India's Competitive Federalism Advantage

• India's greatest strength is competitive federalism - state chief ministers have significant power and compete for investment
• Karnataka emerged as biggest success story, with broad-based growth beyond just Bangalore/IT
• Need more decentralization from center to states, and from states to mayors/local authorities
• Business executives receive calls from five chief ministers when setting up factories

Investment Strategy and Market Outlook

• Small caps in India overvalued; large caps likely to outperform in near term
• International markets already outperforming US in 2024 (20% vs 8-9%), but AI bubble masking US weakness
• Emerging markets and Eastern Europe preferred over US; manufacturing remains key sector for Indian wealth creation
• Dollar expected to weaken significantly over next 5-7 years regardless of reserve currency status
